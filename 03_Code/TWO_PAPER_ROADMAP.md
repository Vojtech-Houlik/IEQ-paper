# Two-Paper Roadmap: Thermal Comfort and IEQ

This roadmap is the shared working map for the two-paper split:

- `thermal_comfort_paper`: thermal comfort paper
- `ieq_paper`: overall IEQ paper
- `../02_Datasets`: central location for clean and model-ready Excel datasets
- `../04_Figures`: final paper-facing figures used directly by the LaTeX article, split into paper-specific subfolders

The goal is to keep the work reproducible, easy to return to, and clear enough to discuss with supervisors. Every substantial step should update the relevant `progress.md`, regenerate/export the PDF version, and keep the executable workflow inside one main notebook per paper.

Recommended starting rule:

- Numeric values: median imputation.
- Numeric missingness: consider explicit missing-value indicator columns when the feature is important and missingness may carry information, but keep them only if the validation gain is large enough to justify the added feature complexity.
- Categorical values: `Unknown` category.
- Keep complete-case counts visible in internal analysis, but decide later whether paper figures should show them.

Current implemented imputation rule:

- Clean datasets are stored in `../02_Datasets/clean`; model-ready imputed datasets are generated separately in `../02_Datasets/model_ready`.
- `_project_tools/create_step1_clean_paper_datasets.py` creates the clean datasets.
- `_project_tools/create_step2_imputed_model_datasets.py` reads the clean datasets and creates the model-ready datasets.
- `Occupant ID`, `Group`, `Subgroup`, and `Student` metadata were added to both clean datasets.
- `Age` is transferred first from matching `Occupant ID` records, then estimated from `Group` + `Student`, `Group`, and global medians.
- `CLO`, `Ttrend`, `Sound`, and `Lighting` use group-aware median rules with broader fallbacks.
- `Gender` is transferred from `Occupant ID` where possible, otherwise assigned by seeded random draw from the observed group distribution.
- Classroom position is retained as the raw `LocationBack`, `LocationFront`, `LocationLeft`, `LocationRight`, and `LocationMiddle` binary flags instead of being collapsed into one classroom-position category.
- Unknown classroom location is defaulted to `LocationMiddle`.
- The model-ready `data` sheets remove trace/context columns (`source_row_number`, `Occupant ID`, `Group`, `Subgroup`) and imputation metadata columns. The `Estimated Age` flag was removed from the paper-facing `data` sheets on 2026-05-13 because it was not needed as a model input; detailed imputation counts remain in the workbook support sheets.
- Broad missingness indicators (`Imputed CLO`, `Imputed Age`, `Imputed Gender`, `Imputed Location`, `Imputed Ttrend`, IEQ-specific `Imputed Lighting`/`Imputed Sound`, and the compact `Estimated Age` flag) were tested and then removed. The fixed-parameter gains were marginal and not robust enough to justify adding these columns to the paper-facing model-ready datasets.
- Class-imbalance strategies were also tested on 2026-05-07. Thermal synthetic sampling, sample-weighted fitting, and probability multiplier calibration did not improve the primary macro F1 result enough to replace the tuned thermal Random Forest baseline. For IEQ, sample weighting and probability multiplier calibration did not beat the refined Extra Trees baseline; earlier IEQ synthetic sampling had also failed to improve macro F1.
- Primary-school-only training was tested on 2026-05-08. It did not improve the best thermal comfort model on primary-school rows. For IEQ, the primary-only Extra Trees model reached accuracy=0.762 and macro F1=0.624, compared with the all-school Extra Trees baseline accuracy=0.725 and macro F1=0.597. The same-population comparison is smaller but still positive: the all-school model evaluated only on primary rows reached accuracy=0.758 and macro F1=0.614. Treat this as a strong IEQ subset/sensitivity result, not as a replacement for the all-school model.
